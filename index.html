KEY INSIGHTS
ABOUT THIS PLATFORM
This platform shows population-weighted exposure to tropospheric NO₂ from TROPOMI/Sentinel-5P satellite observations over West Africa (2020–2024).
The NPWEI scales raw PWE values (molecules/cm²) to a 0–100 index relative to the highest and lowest country values per season, enabling intuitive cross-country comparison.
Only urban pixels (population density ≥ 50 people/km²) are included in the exposure calculation, isolating human exposure from background rural NO₂.
Long-term exposure to NO₂ is associated with respiratory diseases, cardiovascular effects, and increased mortality. This tool supports evidence-based public health decisions.

Why it matters

Long-term NO₂ exposure irritates the lungs and increases risk of respiratory and cardiovascular disease, particularly in dense urban areas.

Who can use this

Policy makers, public health professionals, researchers, environmental agencies, and NGOs working across West Africa.

Data & Methods

TROPOMI/Sentinel-5P monthly NO₂ · NASA Gridded Population · NASA FIRMS · Urban threshold: ≥50 people/km²
