⚙️ Methodology
How We Compute the NPWEI
A four-step pipeline from raw satellite data to a normalised population exposure index
1
Data Acquisition
Monthly TROPOMI NO₂ L3 files (.nc4) downloaded from NASA GES DISC for Jan 2020–Dec 2024. NASA Gridded 2020 population raster clipped to West Africa at 0.1° resolution.
2
Urban Masking
Pixel density computed accounting for latitude-varying cell area. Only pixels ≥50 people/km² contribute — isolating genuine urban exposure from sparse rural areas.
3
PWE Computation
PWE = Σ(population × NO₂) / Σ(population) per city and country. Seasonal means computed for DJF, JJA, and Annual using RegularGridInterpolator alignment.
4
Normalisation & Export
Raw PWE normalised 0–100 per season: NPWEI = (PWE − min)/(max − min) × 100. Exported as JSON and PNG overlay tiles for web rendering.
⚠️ Risk Classification
Understanding the NPWEI Risk Scale
The Normalised Population-Weighted Exposure Index (NPWEI) is scored 0–100 across five tiers. Cities and countries are benchmarked relative to each other within each season — making the index a comparative measure of relative urban NO₂ burden rather than an absolute concentration threshold.

West Africa NO₂ Exposure Risk Platform
An open-access satellite-derived dashboard tracking population-weighted NO₂ exposure across West African urban centres from 2020 to 2024. Built by the Climate and Health Research Lab (CAHL) at KNUST, Kumasi, Ghana.

🎯
Purpose & Objectives
This platform visualises satellite-derived NO₂ column data to estimate urban population exposure across West Africa. It supports researchers, policymakers, and public health practitioners in identifying high-risk areas, seasonal patterns, and city-level burden.
🏛
Research Group
Developed by the Climate and Health Research Lab (CAHL) at KNUST, Kumasi, Ghana. CAHL focuses on climate variability, air quality, and population health across sub-Saharan Africa.

Contact: cahl@knust.edu.gh
📐
NPWEI Definition
The Normalised Population-Weighted Exposure Index (NPWEI):

PWE = Σ(population × NO₂) / Σ(population)Urban pixels ≥50 people/km² only. Normalised 0–100:NPWEI = (PWE − min)/(max − min) × 100
⚠️
Limitations & Disclaimer
TROPOMI measures tropospheric NO₂ columns, not surface concentrations. Results reflect relative exposure differences. Population figures are NASA Gridded Population 2020 estimates. No clinical health outcomes are implied.
⚙️ Processing Methodology
1
Data Acquisition
Monthly TROPOMI NO₂ L3 files downloaded Jan 2020–Dec 2024. NASA Gridded Population 2020 at 0.1° clipped to West Africa.
2
Urban Masking
Only pixels ≥50 people/km² included. Latitude-varying cell area computed.
3
PWE Computation
NO₂ interpolated onto population grid. Seasonal means: DJF, JJA, Annual per city and country.
4
Normalisation
Raw PWE normalised 0–100. Exported as JSON + georeferenced PNG tiles for Leaflet.
